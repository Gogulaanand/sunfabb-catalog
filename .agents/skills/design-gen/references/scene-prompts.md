# Canonical bedspread scene prompts

Use the matching block for each first-colorway master scene. Attach the swatch named by the prep
step and keep landscape 4:3 output. If the prep file or progress tracker records stricter motif
requirements, add them under `Pattern` and `Do not` without removing any rule below.

## Controlled scene-family rotation

Before generating a new design, inspect the two most recently completed designs and select a
family they did not use. Use one family across all four first-colorway masters and record its
letter in `CATALOG_PROGRESS.md`. Family A is the original baseline and applies when no override is
stated. For families B-D, replace only the `Room`/`Set`, `Lighting`, `Camera`, and `Composition`
details in the canonical blocks with the matching guidance below. All `Reference`, `Product`,
`Pattern`, `Output`, and `Do not` rules remain mandatory.

The rotation changes presentation, not brand identity: every family stays bright, minimal,
warm-neutral, softly daylit, uncluttered, photorealistic, and led by the textile. Do not add visual
variety through dark rooms, dramatic lighting, ornate furniture, saturated walls, excessive props,
or competing patterns.

### Family A — light-oak baseline

- Hero: simple light-wood bed; front-left, slightly elevated 50mm view; soft daylight from left.
- Close-up: front corner at mattress height with an 85mm close-focus view; light-wood frame;
  daylight from left.
- Folded: four-layer stack on a light-oak bench; nearly straight-on 50mm view; daylight from right.
- Room: light-wood bed, window and sheers on right, bedside table and ceramic lamp on left;
  eye-level front-left 35mm view.

### Family B — soft upholstered frame

- Hero: low oatmeal-upholstered bed against a lightly textured warm-white wall; one slim pale-oak
  bedside table; front-right, slightly elevated 50mm view; soft daylight from right.
- Close-up: opposite front corner at mattress height, with the oatmeal upholstery subtly visible;
  85mm close-focus view; soft daylight from right and an uncluttered blurred background.
- Folded: four-layer stack on a slim warm-white stone or plaster console against a pale neutral
  wall; slight front-right 50mm view; soft daylight from left; at most one small ceramic vessel.
- Room: oatmeal-upholstered bed, window and sheer curtains on left, pale-oak bedside table and
  simple ceramic lamp on right; eye-level front-right 35mm view with the full bed dominant.

### Family C — cane-and-oak calm

- Hero: restrained pale-oak bed with a simple woven-cane headboard; near-frontal, gently elevated
  50mm view offset slightly left; soft daylight from behind-right.
- Close-up: side drape near the foot of the bed, cane headboard only faintly present in the blurred
  background; low 85mm close-focus view; soft daylight from behind-right.
- Folded: four-layer stack on a pale-oak stool or low console; straight-on 50mm view with more
  horizontal negative space; soft daylight from left; no plant required.
- Room: cane-and-oak bed in a clean warm-white room, a narrow window on the right, one small round
  side table on the left, and one restrained plant; near-frontal eye-level 35mm view.

### Family D — rounded oak platform

- Hero: low bed with a plain rounded pale-oak headboard and softly plastered off-white wall;
  front-right 50mm view at only a slight elevation; soft daylight from high left.
- Close-up: lower view of a side corner near the foot, rounded oak rail subtly visible; 85mm
  close-focus view; soft daylight from high left.
- Folded: four-layer stack on a low rounded-edge pale-oak bench; slight front-left 50mm view; soft
  daylight from right; one small branch in a plain vessel may sit well behind the textile.
- Room: rounded oak platform bed, tall window on left, one floating pale-oak shelf or bedside table
  on right, no lamp required; eye-level front-right 35mm view with generous but balanced space.

## Hero

```text
Reference:
Use the attached fabric swatch as the exact material and print reference.

Product:
Queen-size bed dressed in one bedspread made from this exact fabric, with exactly two matching
pillow covers. Use realistic cotton weight, natural drape, clean hems, and believable folds.

Pattern:
Preserve the uploaded pattern exactly:
- identical motif geometry and count
- identical repeat and spacing
- identical scale and orientation
- identical colors and background color
Apply the print continuously and naturally across the bedspread and both pillow covers without
stretching, mirroring, warping, or changing it at folds and edges.

Room:
Bright minimal bedroom with warm-white walls, light-oak flooring, a simple light-wood bed, and
restrained neutral styling. Keep the textile as the clear focal point. No people or pets.

Lighting:
Soft diffused daylight from a window on the left. Natural soft shadows, neutral white balance,
and no harsh highlights or dramatic color cast.

Camera:
50mm lens
f/4
Slightly elevated three-quarter view from the front-left
Camera level enough to keep vertical lines straight
Entire bed and both matching pillow covers clearly visible

Composition:
Professional catalog hero framing with comfortable space around the bed. Show the front and one
side of the bedspread, including its drape over the mattress edge. Avoid cropped bed corners and
excessive empty wall.

Output:
Ultra-photorealistic professional home-textile catalog photograph. Landscape 4:3 aspect ratio.
Accurate fabric texture, realistic construction, crisp textile detail, and no text or labels.

Do not:
Redesign, simplify, recolor, mirror, rotate, stretch, or distort the pattern.
Add, remove, merge, or substitute motifs.
Invent borders, quilting, embroidery, piping, ruffles, seams, logos, or decorative panels.
Add extra pillows, throws, blankets, or a second textile pattern.
```

```text
Reference:
Use the attached fabric swatch as the exact material and print reference.

Product:

Preserve the uploaded pattern exactly:
- identical motif geometry and count
- identical repeat and spacing
- identical scale and orientation
- identical colors and background color

Lighting:
Soft diffused window light from the left, gentle shadow falloff, neutral white balance, and visible

Camera:
85mm close-focus lens
f/3.2
Close three-quarter view at mattress height
Focus on the corner fold, hem, and nearest printed motifs
Shallow depth of field with the featured print sharply resolved

Composition:
The draped corner fills most of the frame. Include enough mattress edge and bed frame to establish
scale while keeping the print and material detail dominant.

Output:
Ultra-photorealistic professional home-textile catalog detail photograph. Landscape 4:3 aspect

```

## Folded

```text
Reference:
Use the attached fabric swatch as the exact material and print reference.

Product:
One queen-size bedspread made from this exact fabric, neatly folded into a stable stack of four
layers on a light-oak bench. Use realistic cotton thickness, aligned edges, soft compression, and
subtle natural folds. Every visible printed surface belongs to the same bedspread.

Pattern:
Preserve the uploaded pattern exactly:
- identical motif geometry and count
- identical repeat and spacing
- identical scale and orientation
- identical colors and background color
Keep the print continuous and consistently scaled across every visible fold. Do not duplicate or
rearrange motifs merely to center them on the stack.

Set:
Plain warm-white wall, light-oak bench, and light-oak floor. One small restrained green plant may
sit beside the stack, never in front of it. No people, pets, or competing textile products.

Lighting:
Soft diffused daylight from the right, gentle contact shadows, neutral white balance, and even
exposure across the full stack.

Camera:
50mm lens
f/5.6
Straight-on view at stack height with a very slight three-quarter angle
Keep bench and wall lines straight
Entire folded stack in sharp focus

Composition:
Center the stack as the catalog subject with the bench visible beneath it and clean negative space
around it. Show the top and front folded edges clearly; do not crop the stack.

Output:
Ultra-photorealistic professional home-textile catalog product photograph. Landscape 4:3 aspect
ratio. Accurate fabric texture, realistic folds and weight, crisp detail, and no text or labels.

Do not:
Redesign, simplify, recolor, mirror, rotate, stretch, or distort the pattern.
Add, remove, merge, or substitute motifs.
Turn the stack into multiple separate products or add pillow covers, towels, ribbons, packaging,
logos, embroidery, quilting, piping, tassels, or fringe.
```
